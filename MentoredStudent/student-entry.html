Share Your Experience

Your mentoring story matters.

Mentoring here is a partnership. Your faculty treated you as a junior colleague, helped you navigate the unwritten rules of your field, and cared about who you are becoming — not just what you produce. This form is your space to document that.

i. About You

Enter your real name so the department can recognize your contribution. If you'd rather not have your name appear on the public archive, check the box below — you'll be shown as "Anonymous student" instead.

ii. The Mentoring Venue

Where did the mentoring primarily take place?

iii. Part A — A Transformative Moment

Describe the most transformative experience you remember that demonstrates good mentoring. Give details about what happened and the effect it had on you personally. Be as specific as you can — name the moment, the conversation, the feeling.

iv. Part B — The BYU Aims

BYU has four institutional aims. Below you'll find reflection prompts for each aim. Open any aim that resonates with your experience, select the prompt that best matches a specific moment, and describe what happened in the text box that appears. You may engage with as many or as few as feel true to your experience.

Good mentoring at BYU should strengthen your testimony and help you see the connection between your academic work and your faith.

This aim focuses on academic excellence, breadth of knowledge, and the kind of rigorous thinking that turns a student into a scholar.

Mentoring should develop moral virtues through example — in the small acts, the honest admissions, and the professional habits modeled daily.

The final aim focuses on long-term impact — using your skills to serve others and carrying the mentoring relationship forward into your own career.

v. Photo — optional

A photo that captures your mentoring experience — in the lab, at a conference, in the field, or with your mentor. JPG or PNG, up to 4 MB.
