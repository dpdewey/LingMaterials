About this archive
Mentoring as stewardship.

Mentoring at BYU is a partnership focused on your development as a whole person. Faculty act as stewards of your success — treating you as a junior colleague, working side-by-side on projects that solve real-world problems, and caring as much about your well-being and purpose as they do about your scholarship.

In practice, your mentor helps you navigate the unwritten rules of your future career while modeling how to balance professional excellence with deep personal faith. They provide wise feedback — combining high expectations with the personal support you need to meet them — and help you discover what you are capable of through hands-on experience. This archive is where that work gets remembered.
